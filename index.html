Babies

The beginning years of life are vital in a child's development. During this time, the brain is growing at a high speed gathering and retaining as much information as it can through senses: touch, hear, taste, smell, and sight. With this information and knowledge, children are able to build a strong foundation to continue acquiring knowledge as they learn more about the world around them.

Toddlers

As babies grow into toddlers, they deepen their understanding of the world as well as begin to learn how to socially interact with others. They are no longer focusing on their own needs, but begin to be considerate of others. Children will learn how to share, negotiate, compromise, and care for one another during this period of growth. Furthermore, toddlers will expand their academic vocabulary as well as strengthen both their literacy and mathematical skills through play and other structure time in day care. This will prepare them for successful years in school.

School Age

School is a place where children apply skills they have learned and learn new skills that will aid them as they progress through school. There will be times students excel as well as struggle, but no matter what all children have the capability to succeed in school and in life as long as they have the proper support.

For Early Care and Education and School Age Child Care

The Division of Early Childhood Development (DECD) will provide leadership in promoting readiness for school and life for all children, including those with special needs and disabilities. The Division will develop a high quality early care and education system statewide, which provides safe, healthy, nurturing, culturally sensitive, and cognitively stimulating environments for children. Literacy is a significant area children need to obtain and build upon at a young age. By the time children enter school, the expectation is thatchildren need to have a vocabulary bank of at least 10,000 words.For children to attain this vast amount of words prior to beginning school, children need to see, hear, and speak words as often as they can to themselves, to each others, and to adults. To help children achieve this expectation while in my care, children will have access to a variety of books, will be spoken to and taught a wide range of words, and participate in conversations with myself as well as other children.

Mathematics is one of the areas of knowledge that will be used everyday in a child's life. Therefore, it is never too early for children to learn math and begin developing their sense of numbers. To help children strengthen their math skills, children will be asked to do tasks such as count a variety of objects, respond to simple word problems, and solve number sentences as we progress through the day.

Science is everywhere and children in my care will have the opportunity to develop a love for science. Children will learn and explore areas of science such as animals, space, plants, and the air we breath, through outdoor explorations, experiments, and books.

Throughout the week, children will learn and perform different types of art projects using paint, sand, crayons, etc., to express themselves freely. The artwork that is produced will either be sent home for parents to enjoy or hung up on the wall in the day care.

Children will have the opportunity to learn about foods, how to prepare foods, and how to even cook foods that they eat. They will be able to taste a variety of foods, which will help enhance their palette, learn how math and science are incorporated into cooking foods, and how eating healthy is important.

Free play allows children the time to express themselves and create an imaginary world around them. During playtime, children are able to apply and practice different skills, such as how to resolve problems, how to compare, how to express themselves, as well as are able to practice their vocabulary.

OUR LATEST BLOG ENTRIES

Benefits of Free Play

May 20, 2014 By Laura Doherty

Some parents may feel they have to fill their child's day from the time they wake up to the time they go to bed with toys, games, and activities. This is not true because providing children with free choice or play is essential to a child's development for a number of reasons with one being it helps develop a child's brain. During free time, children are learning how to make decisions on their own, share with others, negotiate, and be creative. Furthermore, children are learning the vital skill of being able to entertain themselves, which takes place more so as we grow older. Free play is a time for parents to sit back and watch their children be children.

Cooking with Children

May 20, 2014 By Laura Doherty

Cooking with children is a fun-filled learning experience for children and parents. There is a wealth of knowledge that comes from teaching children cooking from the science behind growing the food, the math it takes to purchase foods, and the process in which it takes to prepare/cook the food. In addition to the academic reasons, there are health benefits as well. Children can be taught about foods and their nutritional value, what is organic and not, and which foods are better choices for our bodies. In the end, the more children are able to learn, explore, and try a variety of foods, the healthier their lives will become.

Reading with Children

May 20, 2014 By Laura Doherty

One simple task to help improve children's love for reading as well as their knowledge of the English language is to take the time to read with them. Study after study has shown the more children are exposed to reading, the more successful they will be in the classroom and eventually in life. As a child learns to read, they are not only learning about the written language, but they are also developing the ability to think abstractly and pronounce words. Every time a child reads a book, no matter if it is the same one over and over again or a brand new one, children are expanding their word knowledge, which is a vital part of succeeding in school. The more words children know prior to entering school, the more successful they will become. Therefore, take the time to read to your child or with your child as frequently as possible because only positive results will come from it.
